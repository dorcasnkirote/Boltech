Ransomware Response Guide

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malicious software designed to block access to a computer system or data until a ransom is paid. It often encrypts files, making them inaccessible to the user.

Impact of Ransomware

  • Loss of critical data.
  • Operational downtime and disruption.
  • Financial loss due to ransom payments.
  • Reputational damage to the affected organization.
  • Legal and compliance challenges.

What to Do When Attacked

If your organization is hit by ransomware, follow these key steps:

  • Immediately isolate the infected system to prevent the spread of the malware.
  • Do not pay the ransom. Payment does not guarantee data recovery.
  • Inform your IT team or incident response group.
  • Contact law enforcement and report the incident.
  • Engage with cybersecurity experts for recovery guidance.

Incident Response Steps

  1. Identify the infection source and the extent of damage.
  2. Isolate affected systems and devices from the network.
  3. Analyze logs to determine the malware variant and attack vector.
  4. Restore data from backups (if available and clean).
  5. Remove the ransomware using antivirus or specialized tools.
  6. Patch vulnerabilities to prevent reinfection.
  7. Communicate the response actions to stakeholders.

Pro Tip:

Ensure your organization has a robust backup strategy in place. Regularly test backups to confirm they can be restored efficiently.

Prevention Tips

  • Keep software and operating systems up-to-date.
  • Train employees to recognize phishing emails and suspicious links.
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication.
  • Deploy endpoint protection solutions and firewalls.
  • Regularly back up critical data and store it offline.
