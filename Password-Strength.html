Password Security Guide

How to Create Strong Passwords

- Use at least 12 characters, including a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
- Avoid using common words, names, or predictable patterns like "password123" or "abc123".
- Replace letters with symbols or numbers (e.g., "P@ssw0rd!" instead of "Password").

Importance of Strong Passwords

Strong passwords protect your accounts and personal information from unauthorized access. Weak passwords can make it easier for hackers to exploit your accounts, leading to data breaches, identity theft, or financial loss.

Tips for Storing Passwords

- Use a reputable password manager to securely store and manage your passwords.
- Avoid writing passwords on sticky notes or storing them in plain text on your computer.
- Regularly update your passwords and never reuse old ones across multiple accounts.

Attacks Associated with Weak Passwords

- Brute Force Attacks: Hackers try every possible combination until they guess your password.
- Phishing Attacks: Scammers trick you into revealing your password through fake emails or websites.
- Credential Stuffing: Cybercriminals use stolen credentials from one breach to access your other accounts.
